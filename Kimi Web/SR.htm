Boston, facing east along its scenic coastline, has always beckoned me with the promise of witnessing a spectacular sunrise. This summer, an astronomy class presented the perfect opportunity to fulfill this desire. Our project focused on observing the shifts in the sunrise point over time, a phenomenon particularly noticeable from Boston's vantage point in the northern hemisphere during the summer months.

I chose Nahant Beach as my observation spot, visiting on May 22, May 28, and June 16 to document the sun's journey. Committing to this project, I arrived at Nahant Beach daily by 4:30 AM, eagerly anticipating the daybreak. The effort was well worth it; the sunrises I witnessed were nothing short of breathtaking, with the early morning sky painting a masterpiece of colors over the Atlantic Ocean.

On May 22nd, used an island in the photo as a reference. The sun rose to the right of the island.

On May 28th, the time the sun could be seen rising over the flat hill. Compared to the last time the sun rose, it moved slightly to the left.

On June 16th, using a small island as a reference, I observed the sun rising to the left of the island. This demonstrated that during the summer in the Northern Hemisphere, the sunrise point gradually moves to the north. For those who wish to witness this natural spectacle, I recommend choosing a clear day to watch a sunrise in Boston. The tranquil beauty of dawn breaking over the horizon is a sight to remember, providing moments of contemplation and admiration for nature's majesty.
