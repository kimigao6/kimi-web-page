In May, my travels took me to The Breakers Estate in Rhode Island, a magnificent testament to the opulence and grandeur of America's Gilded Age. The intricate designs, elaborate decorations, and historical richness of The Breakers offered a glimpse into a bygone era of extravagant living. This visit not only provided a visual feast of architectural and decorative art but also transported me to a time of unparalleled splendor and social prestige.

Upon entering the estate, you are greeted by a large drawing room. The exquisite chandeliers cast a glow that accentuates the castle's golden splendor and impressive height.

This is a dedicated practice room in the castle. The historical piano serves as a reminder of the importance the American Earl family placed on music education.

The study is situated at the end of the corridor. Its simple sofa decoration radiates a sense of calm and majesty. Historically, the family would gather here after dinner to converse and discuss business matters.
