The boardwalk allows visitors to safely explore and get a close-up view of these natural wonders without harming the delicate ecosystem. Steam plumes rising into the clear blue sky hint at the volcanic activity below the earth's crust.

The travertine terraces at Mammoth Hot Springs are among the many geothermal features in Yellowstone National Park. Hot water from beneath the surface passes through limestone, absorbing carbon dioxide to create a mild acid that dissolves the rock. Once this water reaches the surface and the carbon dioxide evaporates, it results in a white, chalky rock known as travertine. This process leads to the formation of the terraces we observe.

This stunning photograph showcases the Grand Prismatic Spring in Yellowstone National Park, the biggest hot spring in the United States and the third biggest globally. From a high vantage point, the hot spring's vivid colors shift from a deep blue core to bright oranges and yellows along its mineral-laden edges, with steam ascending into the sky.
